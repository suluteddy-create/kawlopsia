# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders and business owners (primarily US, Europe, UAE) who are hitting hiring or growth bottlenecks and are evaluating Kawlopsia as a talent/growth partner. They typically arrive from Instagram or a referral, know little about Kawlopsia yet, and are in a credibility-check phase before committing to a call. The job to be done: decide, within one page, whether this is a serious, selective partner worth booking a consultation with — not a service they self-serve into.

## Product Purpose

The Kawlopsia homepage: the public face of a talent/growth bureau that pairs founders with curated Southeast Asian talent and growth execution — positioned explicitly as a bureau that curates access, not an open marketplace. It plants the premise from the wider platform plan (working title "Bureau Talenta Eksklusif"): both sides are selected, not self-served — founders apply for access and get reviewed before a call, talent passes assessment before entering the bench. The homepage's job is to make that selectivity credible and to drive one action: book a discovery call / apply for access. Success = qualified founders booking calls, not raw traffic or generic lead capture.

## Brand Personality

Bold, tegas (assertive/decisive), edgy. Visually anchored to the existing Kawlopsia Instagram identity: near-black surface, high-voltage neon lime/chartreuse accent, heavy serif/slab display headlines against clean sans body text. The voice should read as confident and selective, not corporate-safe and not startup-cute — closer to a bouncer at a door than a SaaS onboarding wizard.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/AI landing page grammar: hero-metric blocks, gradient text, uniform icon-card grids, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section, numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding used by reflex rather than because the content is a real sequence.
- Open freelancer-marketplace feel (Upwork/Fiverr-style): anything that reads as "browse the full list, pick anyone." The page must feel like access is earned/reviewed, never a self-serve directory.

## Design Principles

- **Access is earned, not browsed** — CTAs and copy frame the next step as "apply / request access / book a call," never "sign up free" or "browse talent."
- **Curation over volume** — sell selectivity directly (assessment, review, rejection as a feature) rather than breadth of the talent pool or speed of hiring.
- **Bold contrast as confidence** — commit to the black + neon-lime, heavy-serif-display identity at full strength; no toning it down into a safe corporate palette.
- **Show, don't tell** — lead with specific testimonials/case-study proof over adjectives ("exclusive," "elite") asserted without evidence.
- **One conversion path** — the whole page drives toward a single primary action (book a discovery call / request access); no competing secondary CTAs diluting it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline. The neon-lime-on-black identity needs explicit contrast verification: body text and interactive labels must hit 4.5:1, large display type ≥3:1 — lime-on-black and near-black-on-lime both need checking, not assumed safe just because the brand palette is high-contrast in principle. Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion` given the bold/edgy direction is likely to lean on animation.
